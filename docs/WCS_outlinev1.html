WCS Basketball – Walkthrough & Discussion Outline

Prepared by: [Your Name], [Your LLC Name]

Purpose of Meeting

A friendly, hands-on walkthrough of the new WCS Basketball website — highlighting how it benefits parents, coaches, and the club overall. The goal is to gather feedback, spark ideas, and discuss next steps for transferring ownership and ongoing management through the new LLC.

Meeting Flow (Approx. 10–20 Minutes)

1. Warm-Up & Vision (2–3 min)
  • Start conversationally: “Where do you see WCS going in the next 1, 2, or 5 years?”
  • Ask about long-term goals — growth, community, player development.
  • Briefly tie in how the website reflects and supports that vision.
2. Site Introduction (1–2 min)
  • Show the live site on both desktop and mobile: wcs-basketball-v2.vercel.app
  • Explain that it’s hosted on Vercel and linked to the club’s domain.
  • Mention it’s a Progressive Web App — fast, installable, and always up-to-date.
3. Guided Walkthrough (8–10 min)
  • Home Page: Clean design, strong branding, and community-first tone.
  • Teams Page: Easy to find rosters and coaches; great for new parents.
  • Schedules Page: Color-coded, mobile-ready practice and game calendar.
  • Coaches Dashboard: Demonstrate posting updates, practices, and drills.
  • Practice Drills: Organized library — foundation for shared coaching resources.
  • Show mobile responsiveness to reinforce accessibility.
4. Discussion & Benefits (4–5 min)
  • Parents: Centralized info, reliable updates, easy access on phones.
  • Coaches: Streamlined communication, team updates, and drill sharing.
  • Club: Professional digital presence reflecting 25 years of excellence.

Discuss how this site helps WCS evolve without complicating daily operations.

5. Next Steps (2–3 min)
  • Explain transferring ownership via your LLC and the proposed pricing structure.
  • Float idea: your LLC acts as WCS’s first sponsor by donating the build fee back.
  • Outline simple, low monthly maintenance plan.
  • Ask for feedback before finalizing transfer and setup.

Presentation Tips

  • Keep the tone collaborative and conversational — not formal.
  • Demonstrate live — use both computer and phone.
  • Encourage discussion as you go rather than waiting until the end.
  • Close by emphasizing partnership: “This site was built for WCS — with WCS.”
